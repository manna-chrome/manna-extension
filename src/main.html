The Daily Examen

The Daily Examen is the practice of prayerful reflection to become more aware of God's presence in our daily lives. It was originally designed for the Jesuit Order by St. Ignatius to help followers of Christ see God's hand at work. Throughout this practice, he encouraged people to engage with Jesus like a friend.

Take a moment to find a quiet place and slow down from the busyness of the day.

Become aware of God's presence

Invite God to enter into this space.

List characteristics of God and pray for the Holy Spirit to accompany you in this time of reflection.

If the day seems confusing or a blur to you, take a moment and ask God to bring clarity and understanding.

Review the day with gratitude

Gratitude is a core aspect in our relationship with God.

Walk through your day in the presence of God and note its joys and delights. Focus on the day's gifts. Examine the work that you did and the people you interacted with. What did you receive from these people, and what did you give them? Pay attention to your emotions and the small things. God is in the details.

Looking back on the day, when did you feel closest to God?
Give thanks to God for those moments
Looking back on the day, when did you feel furthest from God or failed to show His love to others?
Ask God for fogiveness and healing in any shortcomings
Look towards tomorrow

Ask God to give you light and discernment for tomorrow's challenges.

Pay attention to any feelings surfacing up as you survey what's coming up. Are you fearful? Cheerful? Apprehensive? Delighted? Turn these feelings into prayer, and seek God's guidance. Pray for understanding and hope.

Conclude with the Lord's Prayer

Our Father in Heaven, hallowed be your name, you kingdom come, your will be done, on earth as it is in Heaven.

Give us today our daily bread.

And forgive us our debts, as we also have forgiven our debtors.

And lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from the evil one.
